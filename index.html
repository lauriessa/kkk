Presyo Lang Yumayaman!

Price Shocks in Essential Food Staples and

Their Impact on Poor Regions in the Philippines

Source: https://www.philstar.com/headlines/2024/07/22/2372075/175-million-filipinos-cant-afford-basic-needs-psa

For millions of Filipinos, the rising cost of basic food is more than just a statistic. It is a daily struggle. Despite national progress, over 17 million people still live below the poverty line and face the constant threat of price volatility. This financial pressure is often exacerbated by external costs, such as fuel inflation. When fuel prices rise, commuters are forced to spend their limited funds on travel rather than essential goods, while the high cost of moving products creates delays in cash flow (Lazaro, 2023). These logistics challenges do not hit every area equally. Because transport costs depend on distance and infrastructure, food prices vary significantly across different regions. This means that a family’s ability to afford a meal often depends entirely on where they live, leaving those in remote or high-cost areas even more vulnerable to hunger.

Why This Matters:

Regional Impact: We analyze why some areas feel price hikes more than others.

Data-Driven Solutions: Our research tracks essential commodities like rice, meat, poultry, fruits, and vegetables to identify market trends.

A Commitment to Change: We provide insights to help build a more food-secure future for every Filipino household.

Here's an overview of our project.

Motivation

Sudden spikes in food prices make essential goods harder for individuals to afford, but these sharp increases do not always happen at the same time across regions. Economically disadvantaged individuals feel the strongest impact, which can deepen existing inequalities.

Our study, Presyo lang Yumayaman, investigates the timing and impact of food price spikes across the Philippines from 2021 to 2025. By understanding how these price shifts move across different regions, we aim to provide the data needed to stabilize markets and protect vulnerable households.

Problem

Frequent food price spikes are a major problem in the Philippines because they happen at different times across regions, making it harder for the poor to recover.

Solution

Our project delivers a deep-dive analysis into how price hikes directly impact poverty across the Philippines. We use the power of data science to understand market trends and identify the best ways to protect the Filipino community from economic shocks.

By comparing these trends with average household incomes, we determine whether Filipino families can truly afford essential food items. This data allows us to identify gaps in the market and develop smarter strategies for a more stable and affordable food system.

The Big Questions: What We Are Solving

To understand how food costs truly impact Filipino families, our research is driven by two critical questions:

1. Does Every Region Feel the Squeeze at the Same Time?

To what extent do the annual price spikes of selected food commodities coincide across regions between 2021 and 2025, and did this synchronization differ between regions with higher and lower 2023 poverty incidence among the population?

2. Is it the Real Poverty Driver?

Is there a significant relationship between regional price volatility from 2021 to 2025 and the region’s poverty index among the population?

Our Big Bets: The Hypotheses We Are Testing

Our research is built on two strategic assumptions that we are testing with data from 2021 to 2025:

  • The Poverty Split: The co-movement of annual surges in selected food items differs significantly across regions and varies according to their 2023 poverty incidence classification.
  • The Volatility Connection: There is a statistically significant association between the magnitude of regional market variability from 2021 to 2025 and the level of deprivation experienced by the population within each region.
Let's take a look at our data

Our data.

This data set contains the following data:

2023 Regional Poverty Incidence among Population

The 2023 Regional Poverty Incidence among Population data came from the Philippine Statistics Authority, which is the government agency responsible for collecting, compiling, and publishing official statistics in the Philippines.

Monthly Regional Food Price For selected Commodity from January 2021 to December 2025

The data for Monthly Regional Food Price For Selected Commodity from January 2021 to December 2025 is sourced from the Humanitarian Data Exchange (HDX), a platform managed by the United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (OCHA). The dataset contains prices of basic commodities from 2000 to 2025, categorized by regions in the Philippines.

The Commodities : Rice, Meat, Beef, Cabbage, Eggplants, Eggs, and Tomatoes

Data Preprocessing

We processed over 91,000 data points from the Humanitarian Data Exchange (HDX) to identify key food price trends across the Philippines. Our preprocessing involved the following steps:

  • Strategic Filtering: Limited the dataset to records from 2021–2025 to reflect the current economic climate.
  • Regional Grouping: Organized the data by region and year to track how price changes moved across the country.
  • Data Intersection: Focused only on commodities consistently recorded across all regions to ensure reliable comparisons.
  • Frequency Ranking: Prioritized commodities based on the Food Consumption Survey from the 2021 Expanded National Nutrition Survey conducted by DOST–FNRI.
