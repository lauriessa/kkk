Presyo Lang Yumayaman!:

Price Shocks in Essential Food Staples and Their Impact on Poor Regions in the Philippines

For millions of Filipinos, the rising cost of basic food is more than just a statistic. It is a daily struggle. Despite national progress, over 17 million people still live below the poverty line and face the constant threat of price volatility.

This financial pressure is often exacerbated by external costs, such as fuel inflation. When fuel prices rise, commuters are forced to spend their limited funds on travel rather than essential goods, while the high cost of moving products creates delays in cash flow (Lazaro, 2023). These logistics challenges do not hit every area equally. Because transport costs depend on distance and infrastructure, food prices vary significantly across different regions. This means that a family’s ability to afford a meal often depends entirely on where they live, leaving those in remote or high-cost areas even more vulnerable to hunger.

Why This Matters:

Regional Impact: We analyze why some areas feel price hikes more than others.

Data-Driven Solutions: Our research tracks essential commodities like rice, meat, poultry, fruits, and vegetables to identify market trends.

A Commitment to Change: We provide insights to help build a more food-secure future for every Filipino household.

Motivation

Sudden spikes in food prices make essential goods harder for individuals to afford, but these sharp increases do not always happen at the same time across regions. Economically disadvantaged individuals feel the strongest impact, which can deepen existing inequalities.

Our study, Presyo lang Yumayaman, investigates the timing and impact of food price spikes across the Philippines from 2021 to 2025. By understanding how these price shifts move across different regions, we aim to provide the data needed to stabilize markets and protect vulnerable households. Our work is dedicated to supporting the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals of Zero Hunger and No Poverty.

Problem

Frequent food price spikes are a major problem in the Philippines because they happen at different times across regions, making it harder for the poor to recover.

Solution

Our project delivers a deep-dive analysis into how price hikes directly impact poverty across the Philippines. We use the power of data science to understand market trends and identify the best ways to protect the Filipino community from economic shocks.

Our approach analyzes food price trends across the Philippines over several years to understand how these shifts impact different areas. By comparing these trends with average household incomes, we determine whether Filipino families can truly afford essential food items. This data allows us to identify gaps in the market and develop smarter strategies for a more stable and affordable food system.

The Big Questions: What We Are Solving

To understand how food costs truly impact Filipino families, our research is driven by two critical questions:

1. Does Every Region Feel the Squeeze at the Same Time?

To what extent do the annual price spikes of selected food commodities coincide across regions between 2021 and 2025, and did this synchronization differ between regions with higher and lower 2023 poverty incidence among the population?

2. Is it the Real Poverty Driver?

Is there a significant relationship between regional price volatility from 2021 to 2025 and the region’s poverty index among the population?
